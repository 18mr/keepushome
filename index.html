Join us in Washington, D.C. April 19!

On April 19, we’ll be in Washington D.C. for the first Adoptee Citizenship Act Day of Action (ACA). Adoptees who don’t have citizenship will share their stories with representatives to emphasize the necessity of the bill’s passage in the Senate and House of Representatives

We can advocate for adoptees by showing up and demanding action from our Members of Congress – regardless of whether or we're adoptees ourselves.

Join 18MillionRising, National Korean American Service and Education Consortium (NAKASEC), Gazillion Strong, and the Adoptee Rights Campaign (ARC) on April 19 to speak with legislators.

When the Child Citizenship Act (CCA) was signed into law in 2000, it granted automatic U.S. citizenship to all international adoptees except those born before January 1983 -- leaving thousands of adoptees at risk of being deported. But the Adoptee Citizenship Act of 2015 (ACA) can rewrite the CCA to grant citizenship to the international adoptees -- and it is on the verge of being passed in Congress.
